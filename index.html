What is Namma Raste?

Bengaluru is at a crossroads today. While it continues to build on its success as an innovation hub and India's Silicon Valley, it needs to rapidly adapt to evolving mobility patterns, driven by new opportunities in the city. In 2023, the Bruhat Bengaluru Mahanagara Palike (BBMP), in collaboration with WRI India, launched Namma Raste (our streets).

  • Namma Raste 2023 focused on safe and climate resilient streets.

  • Namma Raste 2023 took a holistic, data-driven approach to address urban mobility.

Graphics Credits: Arathy Aluckal/WRI India

Namma Raste 2025 was an exploration that served as:

  • A CAMPAIGN exploring Bengaluru's sustainable mobility landscape.
  • An EXHIBITION to experience Bengaluru's public transport transformation.
  • A CONVENING of policymakers and urban designers.
  • A PLATFORM for mobility experts and civic groups.

RESOURCES

Namma Raste Kaipidi(Guidebook); Towards Safe Inclusive, and Resilient Roads in Bengaluru

'Namma Raste Kaipidi' is a guidebook that outlines best practices in design, construction, operation and maintenance of roads. It aims to improve Bengaluru's 12,000 km road network, ensuring that they are designed for long-term sustainability and resilience. This document was prepared as an outcome of the discussions and recommendations under the SOP Committee formed by BBMP under the chairmanship of Engineer in Chief, BBMP.

High Density Corridors Bengaluru.pdf - Design Manual

The High Density Corridor (HDC) initiative reimagines Bengaluru's busiest roads as safer, efficient, and inclusive mobility corridors. This manual, prepared by mayaPRAXIS with BBMP, DULT, and guidance from Transport Planner Rathnakara Reddy, compiles design standards and strategies as a reference for engineers, planners, and communities to create resilient and accessible streets.

Streets and Intersection - Design Checklist for streets and intersection.pdf for infants toddlers and caregivers

An illustrated design checklist and guideline document for creating streets that are safe, inclusive, and engaging for young children and their caregivers. Intended for government agencies and urban practitioners, it provides practical design elements to plan child- and caregiver-friendly streets in cities and neighbourhoods.

Namma Raste Kaipidi Illustrated Handbook

ENGLISH

KANNADA

MOD Foundation, in a first-of-its-kind Illustrated Handbook, bridges the gap between policy and practice, ensuring that Bengaluru's streets are designed to be pedestrian-friendly, utility-smart, and future-ready. The Illustrated Handbook has been adapted from the Namma Raste Kaipidi (Manual), originally prepared as an outcome of the discussions and recommendations under the SOP Committee formed by BBMP office order BBMP/EIC/PR/654/2024-25 dated 19/11/2024.
